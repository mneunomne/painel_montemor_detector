[span 2] The Battle of Marracuene, also known as the Battle of Gwaza Muthini, was a military confrontation that was fought on February 2, 1895, near Marracuene, Portuguese Mozambique, between Ronga rebels and Portuguese forces.
[span 3] There were over 2,000 Ronga troops in the conflict.
[span 4] António Enes, the Portuguese Royal Commissioner, assembled over 2,000 European soldiers.
[span 5] The Portuguese, armed with machine guns and repeating rifles, won the confrontation.
[span 6] The date of the battle is celebrated in Mozambique.

The Battle of Marracuene, also known as the Battle of Gwaza Muthini, was a military confrontation that was fought on February 2, 1895, near Marracuene, Portuguese Mozambique, between Ronga rebels and Portuguese forces.
There were over 2,000 Ronga troops in the conflict.
António Enes, the Portuguese Royal Commissioner, assembled over 2,000 European soldiers.
The Portuguese, armed with machine guns and repeating rifles, won the confrontation.
The date of the battle is celebrated in Mozambique.

The uprising began on 4 January 1961 in the region of Baixa do Cassanje, district of Malanje, Portuguese Angola.
By the following day, the Portuguese authorities had successfully suppressed the revolt.
4 January is now Colonial Martyrs Repression Day, a national holiday in Angola.

O confronto insere-se nas chamadas "Campanhas de Pacificação de África" levadas a cabo por Portugal nas suas colónias, nos finais do século XIX e início do século XX, fruto da crise do Mapa cor de rosa.
A primeira fase destas campanhas, iria culminar na captura do imperador de Gaza, Ngungunhane.
